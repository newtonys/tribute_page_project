David Goggins

the toughest man alive

David Goggins a retired navy SEAL, an ultramarathon runner, and the man who holds the pull-ups world record

Here's a timeline of David Goggins's life

  • 1975 - Born in Williamsville, New York.
  • 1994 - Joined the United States Air Force.
  • 1999 - Left the United States Air Force.
  • 2001 - Became a Navy SEAL.
  • 2004 - Graduated from Army Ranger School.
  • 2005 - Started long-distance running for charity.
  • 2006 - Competed in the Badwater-135 and Ultraman World Championships Triathlon.
  • 2007 - Placed third overall in the Badwater-135.
  • 2008 - Named a "Hero of Running" by Runner's World.
  • 2013 - Set the world record for most pull-ups in 24 hours (4,030).
  • 2016 - Won the Infinitus 88k, Music City Ultra 50k, and Strolling Jim 40 Miler.
  • 2018 - Published "Can't Hurt Me: Master Your Mind and Defy the Odds".
  • 2020 - Ran the Moab 240 ultramarathon.
  • 2022 - Published "Never Finished: Unshackle Your Mind and Win the War Within".
  • 2023 - Broke the world record for most pull-ups in 24 hours (7,801).
